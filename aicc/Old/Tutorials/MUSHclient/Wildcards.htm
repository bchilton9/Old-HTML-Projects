MUSHclient 102 - Wildcards

Wildcards are symbols that represent a group of characters or a single character in your trigger. The * represents a group of characters and the ? represents a single character. The trigger that will be made in this tutorial will be the *. We are going to make a simple bar bot. Click on the Triggers button, then click Add. Type the following in the Trigger textbox:

(* orders *

When someone says, does, or whispers anything, an open parentheses is placed at the beginning of what they say, do, or whisper. The first * represents the Furres name and the second * represents what the Furre ordered. With this trigger, the bot responds to anyone that orders something.

Example: (Red|Dragon orders a root beer

In the Send box, type the following:

:gives %1 %2

%1 represents the first * in the Trigger textbox which in this case is the Furre's name.
%2 represents the second * which in this case is what the furre ordered.

So if Red|Dragon orders a root beer then what the bot sends would like the following:

Example: BotName gives Red|Dragon a root beer

Click OK and close the Triggers window. You now have your first trigger using wildcards!
