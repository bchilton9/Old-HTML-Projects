Respond to Whispers

[1] Your first lesson will be a simple action. Go to the Bot menu and click the Actions option.
On the new window Click the Add Button. You should first name the action with a name that tells you what the action does. Lets name this one 'Greeting', without the quotes, because this action will be used to greet furres when they greet the bot.

[2] We start by by typing your name in the first textbox of the If statement, choosing Whispers from the dropdown listbox, and typing 'hi' without the quotes in the second textbox. The If statement should look like this 'If YourName Whispers hi'.


[3] We won't do anything with the And box just yet so lets move onto the Then box. Click the Add button -under- the Then box. In the new window choose 'Whisper back #1' from the dropdown pulldown menu and type 'hi' in the -small- textbox. Click Update on the current window, click update on the Action window, then click Close and Save your bot. Now when you whisper the bot 'hi' it will whisper 'hi' back to you.




[4] Now a for a slightly more versatile action. Go back to the Bot menu and click Actions again. Name this one 'Relay Message'. Leave the first textbox in the If statement as it is (with an * in it). Choose 'Whispers' from the pulldown menu. Leave the second textbox as it is (with an * in it).


[5] Click the Add button under the Then box and choose 'Whisper #1 to #2' from the pulldown menu. Type '[player] whispered [message] to me.' in the first textbox and type your name in the second textbox. This action deals with variables. 


About FurBot Variables: 

All variables in FurBot are incased in brackets (The brackets are []). The [player] variable holds the value of the whispering furres name and the [message] variable holds the message that the whispering furre sent. There are other variables that can split up the contents of the message. They are as follows:

[startingword#] - All words after and including the # are held in this variable. When used, replace the # with the number of the word you want to start with.
Ex: If * Does orders *
       Then Emote gives [player] [startingword2]

In this example, the bot stores everyword after the second word and the second word in the variable [startingword2]. Then gives the triggering furre what they ordered.
[word#] - A specific word is held in this variable. When used, replace the # with the number of the word you want to be stored in the variable.
Ex: If * Does orders * in a tall glass
      Then Emote gives [player] [word2] in a tall glass.

In this example, the bot stores the second word in the [word2] variable. Then gives the triggering furre what they ordered in a tall glass.

[8] 

Now that you know how to make an action, a Trigger will be easy to learn because Actions and Triggers are only different in one way and that thing is what they are triggered by. Actions are triggered by something a furre does and Triggers are triggered by something Furcadia does.

[9] 

Try making a trigger and have it respond to different things that Furcadia does, for instance, When it connects to Furcadia have it whisper 'Connected' to you. After you have experimented with Triggers you are ready to move onto Timers.

[10] 

Timers are exactly what they seem to be. They do certain things at specified intervals of time. You can have your bot say a certain message every 5 minutes or so. The only problem is that it is only done in seconds so if you want it to do something on an interval thats longer than a minute then you'll have to convert the minutes to seconds. Just multiply the number of minutes you want by 60.

[11] 

Filters can block out any text from showing on the bots screen, being written to the log file, or just not being processed. This can be useful if you have some furre that sets off the same trigger multiple times and you want the bot to stop responding to it.

[12] 

Lastly, is Who Is Online Detection. You can change how often your bot checks to see who is on the map by going to File... Configuration. When the bot checks to see who is on the map is when the Who Is Online Detection triggers take place. It can respond to someone leaving the map, coming onto the map, or to someone staying on the map.
[13]
An example of Who Is Online Detection: 'If * Comes to the map Whisper Hello friend! to [player]'. With this trigger if anyone that is on the current players list that wasn't on it the last time the bot checked to see who is on the map will be whispered 'Hello friend!' by the bot.
